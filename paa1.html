Alan Mathison Turing

Born on June 23, 1912 in Paddington, London, Alan is the the youngest of the two children of Julius Mathison Turing and Ethel Sara Turing. His family belonged to the upper-middle-class, with his father working for the Indian Civil Service (ICS) and his mother as the daughter of the engineer of the Madras Railway.

Alan's mother went to India to reunite with his father when he was about a year old, leaving Alan under the supervision of family friends. He went to St. Michael's in 1918, Hazel Hurst Preparatory School in 1922, and Sherborne School in 1926. He showed a great interest in chess and eventually joined the debating society.

    King's College

    Turing attended King's College from 1931 to 1934, graduating with a degree in Mathematics with first-class honors. He was then elected as a Fellow of the King's College.

    Princeton University

    Turing studied in Princeton University from 1936 to 1938. He earned his Ph.D. in mathematical logic.
